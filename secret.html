Lore mysteries:

Here is a list of lore elements I have as of yet been unable to work out or find out about online:
  • Does Karn have a grudge against Carrow's God Vieris? When the seer from the brotherhood of the watchful eye first meet Karn he says 'get revenge on he who shackled you'. When he says this I believe he is referring to Vieris due to a later encounter in which he tells Karn the after he kills the wicked kings he can 'kill Vieris'. I have watched the scenes with subtitles and the name is spelt the same as Vieris, the 'Lord of mercy and God of light'. What did Vieris do to Karn? Is Karn more than simply a human - he meritted being 'shackled' by a God!
  • What is the exact mechanism by which the wicked kings possess puppets? We never see more than one puppet on screen at a time and scenes involving them normally end with the kings killing the puppet. It is not clear whether the kings are controlling more than one puppet at any point in time, so we cannot assumne they can. At one point Wren comes across a rotting puppet who is still controlled by the kings, at which point they say 'who left this thing on?' showing they can control vessels beyond death and there is some sort of mechanism by which they do so, presumably one which can be easily forgotten is on. It is also seen at one point that they can leave puppets, returning them to their previous owners but in this one case the assassin is instructed to kill the ex-puppet
  • Is the opposite of Thundershock 'exploding citrus or chain lightning lemons'? If not then how does Perfs retrocasting work? It is hinted that he always casts the exact opposite but when enquiring about it, Strong Light Bull initially asks if he casts unintended spells, not necessarily opposites, although after being told it is always the opposite effect, he diagnoses Perf with dyslexia. Does Perf always cast the opposite? Is exploding lemons the opposite of thundershock? If yes then no, what does he cast the opposite of: the spell or his intentions?
